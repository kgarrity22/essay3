The echo of the door as it closed behind him reverberated throughout the apartment, making me acutely aware of the sudden silence he left behind.
When it was going to be all of us, having an itinerary hadn’t mattered; it didn’t matter what we did as long as we were spending time together. But now here alone with Ted, I wished that we had made a minute-to-minute schedule that would carry us through each day.
“Is this it?” I said when the map told us we had arrived. The site, number one recommended by our Airbnb host, appeared to be just a really tall flag in the middle of a traffic circle.
We tried on ridiculous hats in shops and took pictures at landmarks and street signs alike, engaging in the general foolishness people feel comfortable engaging in when they’re an unknown person in a foreign city.
We showed Anna the World’s Lamest Landmark, our flagpole, with theatrical “Ta-da’s” as we recounted the story of our original discovery of it.
Around us, other tourists milled about in a similar silent awe. Standing there looking out over the city in a crowd with an old friend and a new one I felt the fullest sense of togetherness that I had been looking for.
The inside of the house is functional, not fancy. It’s used to having kids rushing through it, tromping sand and seawater and crashing into furniture.
The house has definitely changed over the years. But then again so have I.
Just as I am about to open my mouth to complain again, I hear the boom echo in the distance. The color!
We don bathing suits and flip flops on and grab our towels. It’s unseasonably chilly in Boston for May, but we have a tradition to uphold.
We hop on our bikes and rush down at neck-breaking speeds. At the bottom, we whoop and holler as we zigzag through the street, amazed at how something that seemed so terrifying could result in so much pure joy.
I wonder what that is like for her? Is it a constant reminder of her impending demise? Or did it provide her comfort to know she would be with him? And to know she would have a damn good view.
I agree. We watch the sunset in silence as the sky fades from red to purple to black. The three of us alone together, completely at peace.
. These memories remind me that I have people behind me, people who will always be there to support me. They remind me what I have learned how to be strong and brave. They help show me that it is okay, necessary even to grow and move on.



Kathleen Garrity 2020
