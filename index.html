My Journeys

I've created an interactive photo story to help you all get a better idea of what my essays looked like. You can use the buttons to toggle between photos of Barcelona and photos of Hull. Hover over the images to see a small excerpt from my stories.

The echo of the door as it closed behind him reverberated throughout the apartment, making me acutely aware of the sudden silence he left behind.
When it was going to be all of us, having an itinerary hadn’t mattered; it didn’t matter what we did as long as we were spending time together. But now here alone with Ted, I wished that we had made a minute-to-minute schedule that would carry us through each day.
“Is this it?” I said when the map told us we had arrived. The site, number one recommended by our Airbnb host, appeared to be just a really tall flag in the middle of a traffic circle.
We tried on ridiculous hats in shops and took pictures at landmarks and street signs alike, engaging in the general foolishness people feel comfortable engaging in when they’re an unknown person in a foreign city.
We showed Anna the World’s Lamest Landmark, our flagpole, with theatrical “Ta-da’s” as we recounted the story of our original discovery of it.
Around us, other tourists milled about in a similar silent awe. Standing there looking out over the city in a crowd with an old friend and a new one I felt the fullest sense of togetherness that I had been looking for.
